ABOUT US

Using Chess to make a Difference.

Student Led Chess Association (SLCA) is a non-profit 501(c)(3) organization committed to providing chess education to underdeveloped communities that lack exposure to the sport. Chess is beneficial for developing necessary skills such as memory, focus, and problem-solving.

SLCA's curriculum emphasizes chess education from a student's perspective. We organize charity tournaments and events to raise awareness and encourage a love of chess.

INITIATIVES

C.A.S.T.L.E.
Chess Access and Skill Training for Low-income Education

“Only a life lived for others is a life worthwhile.”
     - Albert Einsten

SLCA aims to introduce the game of chess to marginalized communities, providing them with a unique opportunity to develop essential cognitive skills and critical thinking abilities.

Our workshops serve as a platform for nurturing talent and fostering a sense of belonging and achievement among participants!

SLCA Tournaments
Make Life Awesome

SLCA Frequently holds charity tournaments, both in person and online! Tournaments are a fun way to get to know other players and test your skills, while giving back to your community.

However, not all tournaments are serious or charity-oriented. SLCA holds a weekly online tournament called Wacky Wednesday where players compete in a variety of chess gamemodes.
